# Libresse Brand Visual Language Research

## Executive Summary

Libresse is a global feminine care brand owned by Essity that has undergone a significant brand refresh centered on V-zone confidence. The brand moves away from traditional feminine care stereotypes to position itself as a modern, inclusive, and empowering brand. The visual identity is bold, contemporary, and grounded in confidence—featuring a distinctive pink V and blue-to-cyan gradient rhombus as core visual elements.

---

## Brand Identity Overview

### Core Purpose
Libresse aims to eradicate shame and taboos around women's intimate experiences and create a more understanding, inclusive world. The brand's core mission is to make women feel empowered, supported, and able to live their lives without fear and shame.

### Target Audience
- Women+ of diverse ages, body shapes, sizes, disabilities, skin colors, and hair textures
- Modern, confidence-seeking individuals
- Global audience across 150+ countries

### Brand Philosophy
The brand philosophy rejects traditional "girlish frivolity" in feminine care marketing, instead offering a confident new language that reflects a diverse, evolving category. Libresse believes women should feel proud and confident in their bodies regardless of shape, size, or cultural background.

## Visual Identity

### Logo & Core Design Elements

#### Primary Logo System
The brand features the "Power to the 'V'" design system with two main logo versions:

1. **Power V Logo** - Full-featured logo with prominent pink V
2. **Mini V Logo** - Condensed version for smaller applications

#### Color Composition
- **Primary Color**: Powerful pink V - bold, confident, attention-grabbing
- **Secondary Element**: Blue rhombus (primary shape) with subtle gradient from deep blue to cyan
- The gradient transition from deep blue to cyan represents modernity and movement
- Color placement: The V sits at the heart of the identity, supported by the rhombus

#### Key Design Principles
- **Flexibility**: The distinctive shapes inspire patterns and textures beyond the logo
- **Freedom**: Can be used freely across touchpoints without rigid restrictions or traditional clear space rules
- **Hierarchy**: Always rises to the center of the brand's visual story where it's celebrated
- **Modernity**: Eschews old-fashioned design conventions in favor of contemporary expression

### Color Palette

#### Primary Colors
- **Pink V**: A bold, confident pink that serves as the primary accent color
- **Deep Blue**: The base color of the rhombus shape
- **Cyan**: The lighter gradient end of the rhombus, representing freshness and modernity

#### Application Strategy
- **Period Care Products**: Vibrant, saturated colors to convey effectiveness and confidence
- **Daily Intimate Care**: Lighter, fresher tones to suggest gentle care and daily freshness
- **Range Differentiation**: Variant colors on packaging signify different product benefits
- **Pattern & Texture**: The V and rhombus shapes are used to create textures and patterns across all touchpoints

### Design Language Characteristics

#### Modern & Minimalist
- Clear information hierarchy on packaging
- Efficient use of space without clutter
- Contemporary aesthetic that avoids dated feminine stereotypes
- Clean, geometric shapes (V and rhombus) as foundational elements

#### Bold & Confident
- High-contrast color combinations
- Dominant pink V creates strong visual impact
- Eliminates soft, delicate design elements in favor of geometric strength

#### Flexible & Adaptable
- Shapes can be used as patterns, textures, and decorative elements
- Applicable across digital and physical touchpoints
- Maintains consistency while allowing creative expression
- No rigid positioning requirements—shapes respond to context

## Typography

### Current Information
Specific typeface details for Libresse's brand refresh are not extensively documented in available sources. However, based on the brand's modern, confident positioning, the typography likely follows these principles:

- **Modern Sans Serif**: Aligned with contemporary branding trends
- **Clean & Readable**: Supporting clear information hierarchy on packaging
- **Geometric Influence**: Possibly echoing the geometric nature of the V and rhombus shapes
- **Weight Variation**: Likely uses multiple weights (regular, bold, etc.) for hierarchy

**Recommendation for Design System**: Establish a sans serif typeface system with at least three weights (regular, medium, bold) to support hierarchy across applications.

## Imagery & Visual Style

### Photography & Illustration Approach

#### Diversity & Representation
- **Body Diversity**: Showcasing a wide range of body shapes, sizes, and abilities
- **Age Inclusivity**: Representation across different life stages and age groups
- **Skin Tone Diversity**: Inclusive representation of various ethnicities and skin colors
- **Hair Texture Variety**: Acknowledging diverse hair types and textures
- **Global Perspective**: Imagery reflects the brand's presence in 150+ countries

#### Tone & Aesthetic
- **Authentic & Raw**: Genuine, unpolished representation of women's real experiences
- **Empowered**: Conveying confidence, strength, and capability
- **Modern Feminine**: Celebrating femininity without resorting to stereotypes or "girlish" aesthetics
- **Inclusive**: Showing diverse expressions of womanhood and identity
- **Celebratory**: Imagery that honors and celebrates women's bodies and experiences

#### Campaign Examples
- **"Blood Normal" (2017)**: Groundbreaking campaign showing actual period blood on TV instead of blue liquid—breaking taboos through direct imagery
- **"Pain Stories" (2021)**: Campaign exposing the gender pain gap and raising awareness of endometriosis
- **"Never Just a Period" (2024)**: Global campaign highlighting the dissonance between expectations and reality of menstrual experiences
- **"Vulva Kebaya" Campaign**: Culturally-inspired campaign celebrating diverse representations of femininity and body positivity

### Visual Storytelling
The brand uses imagery to:
- Normalize discussions around menstruation and V-zone health
- Challenge cultural taboos through visual representation
- Create emotional connection through authentic human stories
- Celebrate diverse expressions of femininity and confidence

## Tone of Voice & Brand Messaging

### Core Voice Characteristics
- **Confident & Direct**: Speaks directly about taboo subjects without euphemisms
- **Inclusive & Respectful**: Values every voice and actively engages with community
- **Truth-Telling**: Goes deeper than competitors, moving beyond surface-level messaging
- **Empowering**: Focuses on ability and strength rather than limitations
- **Accessible**: Communicates in clear, understandable language

### Key Messaging Pillars

#### 1. Confidence & Empowerment
- Women should feel proud and confident in their bodies
- Confidence comes from understanding and accepting your body
- No shame in natural bodily functions

#### 2. Breaking Taboos
- Period and intimate care are normal, not embarrassing
- Visual representation of real experiences (blood, diverse bodies)
- Direct language and imagery without euphemisms

#### 3. Diversity & Inclusion
- Every woman's experience is valid and represented
- Celebrates differences in body types, ages, abilities, ethnicities
- Global perspective acknowledging cultural differences
- Inclusive language (women+) to include trans and non-binary individuals

#### 4. Body Positivity
- Your body is worthy of confidence and care
- Different bodies, different needs—all valid
- Protection, comfort, and confidence for all

### Messaging Strategy
- **Target Pain Points**: Addresses actual concerns (leaks, odor, discomfort, confidence)
- **Educational**: Provides information to build understanding
- **Validating**: Acknowledges real experiences and challenges
- **Inspirational**: Encourages women to live without fear or shame

## Design System Recommendations

### For Building an On-Brand App

#### Color System
- **Primary**: Pink V accent color (RGB/Hex to be determined from brand assets)
- **Secondary**: Deep blue (RGB/Hex)
- **Tertiary**: Cyan/Light blue gradient (RGB/Hex)
- **Backgrounds**: Likely white or very light neutral tones
- **Text**: Dark blue or black for primary content

#### Typography System
- **Display/Headlines**: Bold, modern sans serif
- **Body Copy**: Regular weight modern sans serif
- **Accent/Small**: Medium weight sans serif
- **Recommend**: At least 3-4 weight options for hierarchy

#### Component Style
- **Geometric**: Incorporate V and rhombus shapes into UI components
- **Clean**: Minimize ornamentation, favor function and clarity
- **Accessible**: High contrast, readable, inclusive
- **Modern**: Contemporary layout patterns, avoid dated elements

#### Imagery Guidelines
- **Diverse Cast**: Represent various body types, ages, abilities, ethnicities
- **Authentic**: Show real moments, not overly styled or polished
- **Confident**: Strong poses, direct eye contact, proud expressions
- **Educational**: Images that normalize and inform, not shame

#### Tone Guidelines for App Copy
- Direct and honest (avoid euphemisms)
- Empowering (focus on capability and confidence)
- Inclusive (acknowledge diverse needs and experiences)
- Educational (help users understand their bodies)
- Respectful (acknowledge sensitivity while being straightforward)
