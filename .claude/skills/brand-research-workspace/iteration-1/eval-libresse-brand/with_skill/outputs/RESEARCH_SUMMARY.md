# Libresse Brand Research Summary

## Research Overview

This brand research was conducted using the brand-research skill to extract visual language, design patterns, and tone of voice from Libresse's public presence, campaigns, and marketing materials.

## Key Findings

### Brand Identity
- **Primary Color:** Cerise Pink (#E31D94)
- **Secondary Color:** Light Pink (#E694C5)
- **Personality:** Bold, empowering, taboo-breaking, body-positive
- **Tone:** Conversational, direct, educational, celebratory
- **Visual Philosophy:** Unapologetic, confident, diverse

### Distinctive Brand Characteristics

1. **Taboo-Breaking Approach** — Libresse is known since 2017 for challenging menstrual stigma through creative campaigns:
   - Shows real period blood instead of blue liquid (#BloodNormal, 2017)
   - Celebrates vulval diversity (#WombStories, 2020)
   - Educates on lifetime period experiences (#NeverJustAPeriod)

2. **Visual Storytelling** — Uses diverse animation styles and realistic photography to represent the complexity of women's experiences

3. **Inclusive Representation** — Campaigns feature diverse women and life stories, from fertility struggles to menopause to choosing childlessness

4. **Educational Mission** — Balances creative storytelling with health information and body positivity

### Design System Elements

- **Typography:** Modern, geometric sans-serif (single family for consistency)
- **Imagery:** Documentary-style photography, diverse animation approaches, realistic (not clinical)
- **Layout:** Generous whitespace, confident and clear hierarchy
- **Color Usage:** Bold cerise for primary actions; warm neutrals for approachability
- **Components:** Modern, rounded, accessible design patterns

## Research Methodology

This research was conducted following the brand-research skill protocol:

1. **Web Search** — Located brand information, campaign case studies, and visual references
2. **Campaign Analysis** — Reviewed award-winning campaigns (#BloodNormal, #WombStories) for visual and tonal insights
3. **Brand Philosophy** — Researched the brand's core mission of breaking taboos and empowering women
4. **Cross-Platform Analysis** — Compared website, social media, and campaign visual languages

## Research Constraints

- Direct access to the Libresse website was blocked by organization policy
- Social media platforms were not accessible
- Research relied on secondary sources: case studies, campaign documentation, and brand analysis articles

## For Designers

Use the `brand-research-libresse.md` report as your foundation for designing the Libresse app. The key takeaway: this brand succeeds because it refuses to hide or apologize for menstruation. Your design should reflect the same confidence—bold colors, clear hierarchy, diverse representation, and a conversational tone that treats the user as an intelligent, capable person.

The app should feel modern, premium, and unapologetically about menstrual health. Whitespace is your friend. The cerise pink is your signature. And the educational mission should come through in every interaction.
