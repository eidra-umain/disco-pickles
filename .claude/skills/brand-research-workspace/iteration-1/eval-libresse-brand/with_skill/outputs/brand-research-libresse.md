## Executive Summary

Libresse is a bold, purpose-driven feminine care brand that positions itself as a taboo-breaker and women's empowerment champion. Rather than hiding behind euphemisms, Libresse uses vibrant visual storytelling—from realistic blood imagery to diverse animation styles—to normalize menstruation and create shame-free conversations around women's bodies. The brand's visual language is modern, confident, and emotionally resonant, combining a bold cerise pink primary color with varied visual styles that reflect the complexity and diversity of women's experiences. The tone is conversational, empowering, and unapologetically body-positive.

## Design Implications

### For App Design

1. **Use bold cerise as the hero color** — it signals confidence and visibility. This is the brand's signature; don't soften it. Use it for primary CTAs, important actions, and accent elements.

2. **Embrace whitespace and breathing room** — the brand's visual language values clarity and confidence over busyness. Give content room to breathe. This creates an accessible, premium feel.

3. **Typography and hierarchy must be clear** — use a single, modern sans-serif family consistently. Establish a strong visual hierarchy so users understand what's most important. The educational mission requires clear communication.

4. **Imagery should be realistic and diverse** — if the app includes photography or illustration, ensure it represents diverse bodies and experiences. This reinforces the brand's core mission of normalizing menstruation and celebrating body diversity.

5. **Tone matters deeply** — the copy should be conversational, empowering, and direct. Avoid clinical or medicalized language unless necessary. The brand breaks taboos through language choices; replicate this in the app.

6. **Animation can tell stories** — if the app includes educational or onboarding content, consider diverse animation styles to make complex information engaging and emotionally resonant (inspired by Womb Stories approach).

7. **Don't apologize for the topic** — the brand's strength lies in its unapologetic approach to menstruation. The app design should reflect the same confidence. Avoid muted colors, clinical layouts, or overly decorative elements that soften the message.
