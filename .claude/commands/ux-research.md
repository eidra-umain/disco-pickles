# UX Research

Deep UX research skill for analyzing target audiences, competitive landscapes, user pain points, and market opportunities. Two modes: **generate** (create new research from a brief) or **validate** (audit and improve existing research).

## Guidelines

### Research Framework — 8 Pillars

1. **Target Audience Analysis** — Demographics, psychographics, device behavior, app usage patterns, aesthetic preferences, notification tolerance, privacy attitudes, subscription fatigue, widget/touchpoint habits
2. **Competitive Landscape** — Direct and adjacent competitors with strengths, weaknesses, pricing, ratings, downloads, monetization models, trust signals
3. **User Pain Points** — Ranked by severity (critical/high/moderate), sourced from app reviews, Reddit/forums, studies, and journalism
4. **Real User Needs** — What users actually want vs what apps provide. Unmet needs. Functional, emotional, and social needs
5. **Business Needs & Opportunities** — Revenue models, strategic positioning, competitive moats, user-business value alignment
6. **Ideation** — Concrete concepts meeting both user and business needs. Feature concepts, UX patterns, interaction models
7. **Market & Sales Opportunities** — Market sizing, growth projections, adjacent revenue, partnership opportunities. Natural sales angles
8. **Hypothesis Validation** — For each hypothesis, assess research support and what prototypes should test

### Quality Standards

- Every claim supported by data, source, or marked as inference
- Statistics include source and date
- Competitor data current within 12 months
- Pain points ranked not just listed
- Ideation connects to specific findings
- Business opportunities include sizing estimates

### Validate Mode Checklist

When validating existing research check:
- **Completeness** — All 8 pillars covered? Gaps?
- **Accuracy** — Stats current? Claims sourced? Outdated data?
- **Depth** — Beyond obvious observations? Genuinely insightful?
- **Actionability** — Can a design team act on this?
- **Balance** — Multiple perspectives? Uncertainties acknowledged?
- **Bias** — Assumptions challenged? Edge cases (accessibility, inclusivity)?
- **Source diversity** — Quantitative + qualitative, industry + user voice?
- **Freshness** — Flag data older than 12 months
- **Missing angles** — Unexplored research areas?
- **Contradictions** — Internal conflicts?

After validation produce audit report and update research with improvements.

## Prerequisites

A project brief or research document in `design/` folder.

## Output

- New research: `design/UX-Research-[Project-Name].md`
- Validation: `design/UX-Research-Validation-Report.md` + updates to original
