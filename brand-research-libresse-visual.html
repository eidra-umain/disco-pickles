05 — Voice
Tone of Voice
Conversational, empowering, and unapologetically direct.

Direct & Honest

Uses "period", "blood", "vulva" without euphemism. Punchy statements mixed with educational content.

Empowering

Focus on ability, strength, and confidence. "Live fearless" — celebratory without being preachy.

Inclusive

Every woman's experience is valid. Uses "women+" to include trans and non-binary individuals.

Conversational

Direct address ("you", "your") for personal connection. Occasionally irreverent or playful.

Brand voice does

  • Say "period" and "blood" directly
  • Celebrate body diversity
  • Use confident, short statements
  • Educate while empowering
  • Show real experiences, real blood

Brand voice avoids

  • Euphemisms ("that time of the month")
  • Clinical or medicalized language
  • Apologetic or muted messaging
  • "Girlish frivolity" stereotypes
  • Blue liquid imagery
06 — Campaigns
Key Campaigns
Award-winning creative that defines the brand's visual storytelling approach.
2017

#BloodNormal

First brand to show real period blood in advertising, replacing the industry-standard blue liquid. Documentary-style footage directed by Daniel Wolfe with cinematography by Monika Lenczewska. Agency: AMV BBDO.

Glass Lion Grand Prix — Cannes
2020

#WombStories

Multiple animation styles representing diverse women's experiences — endometriosis, infertility, menopause, choice. Each story rendered in a unique visual approach: 2D cell, stop-frame, oil-on-glass. Directed by Nisha Ganatra, animated by Framestore.

4x Grand Prix — Cannes
2024

#NeverJustAPeriod

Global campaign highlighting the gap between expectations and reality of menstrual experiences. Educational and validating — acknowledges the lifetime of confusion from first to last period.

07 — For the App
Design Implications
How to translate the brand language into the Libresse app design.
1

Bold cerise as hero color

Use #E31D94 for primary CTAs, important actions, accent elements. Don't soften it — the boldness signals confidence.

2

Generous whitespace

The brand values clarity over busyness. Give content breathing room — communicates "we're not ashamed of this topic."

3

Clear type hierarchy

Single modern sans-serif family. Strong visual hierarchy so the educational mission communicates clearly.

4

Diverse, realistic imagery

Represent diverse bodies and experiences in any photography or illustration. Normalize, don't sanitize.

5

Conversational, empowering copy

Avoid clinical language. Be direct, use real words, break taboos through language choices in the app.

6

Animation for storytelling

Use diverse animation styles for educational/onboarding content. Inspired by the Womb Stories approach.

7

Don't apologize for the topic

The app should reflect the same confidence as the brand. No muted colors, no clinical layouts, no softening.
