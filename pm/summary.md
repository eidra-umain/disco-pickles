# Project Summary

## What
A Libresse-owned mobile app that combines cycle tracking, personalized health insights, and direct-to-consumer product commerce into a single privacy-first platform.

## Why
Libresse (Essity) has strong brand trust across Europe but no owned digital touchpoint — the customer relationship ends at retail purchase. The femtech market is valued at $2.5B and growing, yet the leading period app (Flo) has a 1.6-star rating and was fined $56M for data privacy violations. Users are frustrated by paywalls, invasive data practices, and a disconnect between health insights and the products they need. No trusted consumer brand has stepped in to fill this gap.

## Who
- **The Everyday User** — Woman aged 16–25, urban or suburban, high smartphone adoption. Likely already using Flo, Clue, or Apple Health and dissatisfied. Wants accurate cycle predictions and symptom tracking but is wary of apps that monetise health data. Trusts established brands over tech startups.
- **The Loyal Libresse Customer** — Already buys Libresse products and has a positive brand relationship. Open to subscription if value is clear. A natural early adopter and advocate if the experience delivers.

## Scope
Four core sections: Home (dashboard), My Cycle (tracking), Shop (contextual commerce), Health Assistant (AI-powered Q&A).

## Key Features
- Cycle-phase-aware dashboard with health nudges
- On-device symptom/mood/flow tracking (no data leaves the phone)
- Contextual product recommendations tied to cycle phase
- AI health assistant that answers questions in plain language
- Subscription management and automated reordering

## Open Questions
- Integration with Apple Health / Google Fit
- Specific privacy architecture (on-device ML models vs. encrypted cloud)
- Subscription pricing model
- Regulatory considerations for health advice in different EU markets
