# Project Summary: Libresse Cycle Companion

> A privacy-first period tracking app from Libresse (Essity) that bridges health insights with product commerce.

---

## 1. Problem Statement

### Market Context
The global femtech market is valued at **$2.5B and growing**, yet remains poorly served. The leading period tracking app, Flo, carries a **1.6-star rating** and was fined **$56M by the FTC** for data privacy violations. Users are frustrated by aggressive paywalls, invasive data practices, and a complete disconnect between health insights and the products they actually need.

### The Gap
Despite strong market demand, **no trusted consumer brand has stepped in** to fill this gap. Libresse (Essity) has:
- Strong market presence across Europe
- 100+ years of feminine care expertise
- Existing customer trust
- Direct-to-consumer commerce capability

Yet Libresse has **no owned digital touchpoint** with end users — the relationship stops at retail purchase.

### The Opportunity
Combine genuine product expertise, existing customer trust, and a direct-to-consumer commerce model into a single **privacy-first platform**.

## 6. Workshop Prototype Scope

### Timeline
**4 hours** — Rapid prototype for workshop validation

### In Scope
- [ ] Period tracking UI
  - Calendar view
  - Log period start/end
  - Cycle predictions display

### Explicitly Out of Scope
- Backend/real data persistence (mock data only)
- Real payment processing
- User authentication
- Push notifications
- Real Essity system integration

### Prototype Goal
Produce something **tangible for the client** — a working prototype that demonstrates the core experience and opens a concrete conversation about product direction and commercial model.
